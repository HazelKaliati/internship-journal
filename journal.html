My Internship Experience

Starting the Internship

When I first started my internship at Faith Health, I didn’t really know what to expect. I was both excited and nervous because everything was new to me, especially in a healthcare IT environment.

Computer Imaging & Deployment

One of our first major tasks was re-imaging computers. This included using a boot drive, accessing the boot menu, and deploying systems configured for the organization. At first, it felt overwhelming because I had never done anything like it before.

However, we followed detailed step-by-step documentation in OneNote, which helped a lot. Over time, I became more confident and started understanding the process instead of just following instructions blindly.

Active Directory & Support Tasks

As time went on, we were given more responsibilities. One of the main systems we worked with was Active Directory, where we handled password resets and account unlocks. These became some of the most common support tickets we worked on daily.

Field Support Experience

We also got hands-on experience in field support. This included setting up workstations, moving computers between departments, and helping during office relocations. It showed me that IT is not just behind a desk — it also involves physical, real-world work.
